Hey Hi Hello!

I’ve always liked figuring out how things work.

Not just in the casual “that’s neat” way, but in the slightly obsessive, open-too-many-tabs, take-it-apart-and-see-what-happens way. Technology, creative tools, software, weird ideas for apps, systems for coordinating people, troubleshooting hardware... stuff like that.

This blog is basically a home for that curiosity.

A lot of my interests live at the intersection of tech, creativity, and experimentation. One day that might mean digging into app ideas (like building simple tools that help people coordinate plans or activities without the usual awkward back-and-forth). Another day it might be exploring platforms and features, figuring out what tools can actually do versus what the marketing says they can do.

I’m also pretty involved in creative spaces especially photography and recently exploring several different creative and artistic mediums. I like the technical side of it as much as the artistic side: gear, workflows, resources, learning materials, and ways to make creative groups or communities run better.

So you’ll probably see posts about things like:

  • Random tech problems and the rabbit holes required to solve them
  • App concepts and experiments (especially simple but clever ones)
  • Tools for creators like cameras, editing, learning resources, and workflows
  • Observations about software, platforms, and how they’re evolving
  • Occasional “I went down a weird research path and here’s what I found” moments

I tend to approach things with a mix of curiosity and skepticism. If a feature exists, I want to know what it really does, where the limits are, and what interesting things might happen if you push on those edges a little.

Mostly though, this is just a place to think out loud, document experiments, and share the occasional useful discovery along the way.

If you like digging into tech, creative tools, and half-formed ideas that might turn into something interesting, you’ll probably feel at home here.

 

-Justen
